\chapter{Induction}\label{chap:Induction}

\epigraphhead[70pt]{
\epigraph{Every time that a human being succeeds in making an effort of attention with the sole idea of increasing [their] grasp of truth, [they acquire] a greater aptitude for grasping it, even if [their] effort produces no visible fruit.}{Simone Weil}}

In this chapter, we introduce mathematical induction, which is a proof technique that is useful for proving statements of the form $(\forall n \in \mathbb{N})P(n)$, or more generally $(\forall n \in \mathbb{Z})(n\geq a\implies P(n))$, where $P(n)$ is some predicate and $a \in \mathbb{Z}$.
